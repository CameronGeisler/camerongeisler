Theorem 5.4.8.

Let \(A\) be an \(n \times n\) matrix. Then, the following are each equivalent to the statement that \(A\) is invertible:

  1. The columns of \(A\) form a basis of \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{.}\)
  2. The columns of \(A\) span \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{,}\) \(\Col{A} = \mathbb{R}^n\text{.}\)
  3. \(\dim{\Col{A}} = n\text{.}\)
  4. \(A\) has full rank, \(\rank{A} = n\text{.}\)
  5. \(N(A) = \set{\vec{0}}\text{.}\)
  6. \(\dim{N(A)} = 0\text{.}\)
