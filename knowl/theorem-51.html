Theorem 10.1.7.

Let \(\vec{u}, \vec{v}, \vec{w}\) be vectors in \(\mathbb{R}^n\text{.}\) Then,

  1. Commutative property, \(\vec{u} \bullet \vec{v} = \vec{v} \bullet \vec{u}\text{.}\)
  2. Distributive property, \(\vec{u} \bullet (\vec{v} + \vec{w}) = \vec{u} \bullet \vec{v} + \vec{u} \bullet \vec{w}\text{.}\)
  3. Associativity with scalar multiplication, for \(k \in \mathbb{R}\text{,}\)
    \begin{equation*} (k \vec{u}) \bullet \vec{v} = \vec{u} \bullet (k\vec{v}) = k(\vec{u} \bullet \vec{v}) \end{equation*}
