Theorem 2.6.5.

Let \(A\) be an \(m \times n\) matrix. Then, the following are equivalent:

  1. The equation \(A\vec{x} = \vec{b}\) has a solution for every \(\vec{b} \in \mathbb{R}^m\)
  2. Every \(\vec{b} \in \mathbb{R}^m\) can be written as a linear combination of the columns of \(A\)
  3. The matrix \(A\) has a pivot position in every row.
