If \(\vec{u}, \vec{v} \in \mathbb{R}^2\) are non-zero vector, and \(\vec{v}\) is not a multiple of \(\vec{v}\text{,}\) then \(\Span{\vec{u}, \vec{v}}\) makes up the entire plane \(\mathbb{R}^2\text{.}\) In \(\mathbb{R}^3\text{,}\) \(\Span{\vec{u}, \vec{v}}\) consists of the plane in \(\mathbb{R}^3\) which contains \(\vec{u}, \vec{v}\) and \(\vec{0}\text{.}\)
