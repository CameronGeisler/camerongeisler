Example 2.5.11.
Consider the linear system,
\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rrrr} x \amp + 3y \amp = -1 \\ -5x \amp + 2y \amp = 2 \end{array} \end{equation*}
which has vector form,
\begin{equation*} x \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ -5 \end{bmatrix} + y \begin{bmatrix} 3 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -1 \\ 2 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}
This equation can be thought of as trying to find a linear combination of the vectors \((1, -5)\) and \((3, 2)\) in the plane, that is equal to the vector \((-1, 2)\text{.}\)
