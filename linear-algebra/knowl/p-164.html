From another perspective, by first starting with all points \((x,y,z)\) in 3-dimensional space \(\mathbb{R}^3\text{,}\) and then by requiring the constraint \(ax + by + cz = d\text{,}\) this constraint generally acts to reduce the number of degrees of freedom by 1, i.e. the result is a 2-dimensional object. It turns out that if this equation is linear (like the equation of a plane is), then the result will be a 2-dimensional flat surface, i.e. a plane.
