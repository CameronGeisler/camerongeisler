Theorem 3.3.7.

Let \(\vec{u}, \vec{v}, \vec{w} \in \mathbb{R}^3\) be vectors. Then,

  1. \(\vec{u} \bullet \vec{v} = \vec{v} \bullet \vec{u}\text{.}\)

  2. \(\vec{u} \bullet (\vec{v} + \vec{w}) = \vec{u} \bullet \vec{v} + \vec{u} \bullet \vec{w}\text{.}\)

  3. For \(k \in \mathbb{R}\text{,}\)

    \begin{equation*} (k \vec{u}) \bullet \vec{v} = \vec{u} \bullet (k\vec{v}) = k(\vec{u} \bullet \vec{v}) \end{equation*}

  4. \(\displaystyle \vec{u} \bullet \vec{u} = \abs{\vec{u}}^2\)
