Theorem 5.5.5.

Let \(A\) be an \(m \times n\) matrix, \(B\) and \(C\) be \(n \times p\) matrices, \(D\) and \(E\) be \(q \times m\) matrices, \(a \in \mathbb{R}\) be a scalar. Then,
  1. Multiplication distributes over addition.
    \begin{equation*} A(B + C) = AB + AC \qquad \text{and} \qquad (D + E)A = DA + EA \end{equation*}
  2. Associative with scalar multiplication.
    \begin{equation*} a(AB) = (aA)B = A(aB) \end{equation*}
  3. Associative with matrix multiplication.
    \begin{equation*} A(BC) = (AB)C \end{equation*}
